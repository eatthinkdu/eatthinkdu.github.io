<h3 class="section-heading">Dudes. I fell in love with this country.</h3>

With its vibrant energy, the organized chaos, the frenzied craziness of the locals (the Vietnamese are insane), stepping through swarms of motorbikes hoping you'd make it out unscathed.

<center>
  <img src="{{site.url}}/img/think/vietnam/highlights/streets.JPG" height="300px" width="600px" style="padding:20px;display:inline-block"/>
  <p style="margin-top:-10px;padding-bottom:20px;"><i>Saigon at sunset.</i></p>
</center>

With the constantly changing yet consistently beautiful terrain, the diversity and richness of things to do and places to explore. Beaches, mountains, rice paddies, caves, jungles, waterfalls.

With putting lime on all my noodles, hunting for the cheapest <i>banh mi</i>, sipping on a perfect cup of coffee with condensed milk every morning (and afternoon...and evening...).

You could <i>feel</i> the life pulsing through the streets.

<center>
  <img src="{{site.url}}/img/think/vietnam/highlights/pho.JPG" height="300px" width="600px" style="padding:20px;display:inline-block"/>
  <p style="margin-top:-10px;padding-bottom:20px"><i>A perfect bowl of pho.</i></p>
</center>

<h3>And I loved who I was while I was there.</h3>

The more time I spent in Vietnam, the more I became consumed by it and by the backpacker lifestyle, the more I just <b><i>let. it. happen.</i></b> In between adventuring, I'll remember the countless rounds of pool, games of cards won and lost, bonds forged through stories swapped, mounds of inside jokes shared between groups from all over the world, happy hours that passed like minutes.

I only have fond memories from my time there -- to be honest, I'm even a little sad my visa expired and I'm already in Thailand. I'll be catching up on blogging sometime soon, but for now, just some of my favorite moments from my time in Vietnam.
