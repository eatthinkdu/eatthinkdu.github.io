<h3 class="section-heading">Cambodia.</h3>

It's got the chill beach vibes of south Thailand, the cultural vibes of Vietnam, and can still hold its own with its own national gems -- all without the high profile.

You still got the drunk tourists (why so many Brits?) on gap yah and holiday, restaurants serving Western food (why do I need a taco in Cambodia?), overpriced everything, and the beginnings of infrastructure built to cater to the influx of foreigners coming in.

But there's still the opportunity to see a piece of Cambodia that takes only a tad more time and effort to get to, the places where English is a bit of a struggle, you're staring at rice paddies more than buildings, the dirt on the roads congeal into mud to make for a crazy ride.

Wish I had more time to spend it in the latter and get off the beaten path, because Cambodia truly is a beautiful country with a rich history, open-hearted people, and stunning sights.
