<h3 class="section-heading">Cambodia.</h3>

It's got the chill beach vibes of south Thailand, the cultural vibes of Vietnam, and can still hold its own with its national relics -- all without the high profile.

You still got the drunk tourists on holiday (gap yah anyone?), restaurants serving Western food (why do I need a taco in Cambodia?), overpriced everything, and the beginnings of infrastructure built to cater to the influx of foreigners coming in (I have never used so many $1 bills).

But there's still the opportunity to see a piece of Cambodia that takes only a tad more time and effort to get to, the places where you gesture wildly because English is a bit of a struggle, where you're staring at rice paddies more than buildings, and where the dirt on the countryside roads congeal into mud to make for a crazy motorbike ride. 

Wish I had more time to spend it in the latter and get off the beaten path, because Cambodia truly is a developing country on the brink of a tourism boom, with a rich legacy, open-hearted people, and a nostalgic sort of beauty.
