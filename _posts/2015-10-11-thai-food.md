<h3 class="section-heading">When it comes to Southeast Asian cuisine, Thailand is the clear winner, hands-down, not even a competition.</h3>

Basically, Thailand has pretty kick-ass food. Plentiful coconut milk, palm sugar, throw in a little bit of spice and everything nice - maybe basil, garlic, tumeric. I even took a day-long cooking course in order to try my hand at some of the more popular dishes.

<p style="margin-top:-10px;padding-bottom:20px"><i>Intense focus making curry powder, followed by a successful pad thai.</i></p></center>

<center><img src="{{site.url}}/img/eat/thailand/spices.JPG" width="500px" style="padding:20px;display:inline-block"/><p style="margin-top:-10px;padding-bottom:20px"><i>Basketful of goodness procured by our cooking instructor.</i></p></center>

With the same strain of flavors bringing everything together, you really can't go wrong. Curries, pad thais, fried noodles, sticky rice, papaya salads, refreshing mango shakes. Obtained from the most unassuming of wooden stands off the side of the road, always made by adorable old Thai men and women. I could go on forever (and eat forever) but I don't want you guys to get bored, and I also don't want to come home obese.
